# Protocol Evidence Sources

Evidence sources behind Otto's health recommendations. Evidence levels follow standard grading:

- **A** — Strong evidence from multiple randomized controlled trials or meta-analyses
- **B** — Moderate evidence from at least one RCT or well-designed observational studies
- **C** — Limited evidence from observational studies or expert consensus
- **D** — Expert opinion or case reports

## Cardiovascular / Lipids

### LDL-C Reduction

- **Mediterranean diet**: Estruch et al., 2018. PREDIMED trial. N Engl J Med. 10-15% LDL reduction. [Evidence: A]
- **Psyllium fiber**: Anderson et al., 2000. Am J Clin Nutr. 7% LDL reduction at 10g/day. [Evidence: A]
- **Aerobic exercise**: Mann et al., 2014. J Am Heart Assoc. 5-10% LDL reduction. [Evidence: A]
- **AHA/ACC 2018 Guidelines**: Grundy et al. Cholesterol Clinical Practice Guidelines.

### HDL Improvement

- **Vigorous exercise**: Kodama et al., 2007. Arch Intern Med. Meta-analysis: +2.5 mg/dL per additional weekly exercise session. [Evidence: A]
- **Healthy fats**: Estruch et al., 2018. PREDIMED. Olive oil + nuts arm: +3-4 mg/dL HDL. [Evidence: A]

### Triglyceride Reduction

- **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)**: Skulas-Ray et al., 2019. AHA Science Advisory. 2-4g/day: 20-30% TG reduction. [Evidence: A]
- **Alcohol/sugar reduction**: AHA/ACC 2018. First-line lifestyle intervention. [Evidence: A]

## Metabolic Health

### Glucose / HbA1c

- **Low glycemic index diet**: ADA Standards of Medical Care, 2024. [Evidence: A]
- **Post-meal walking**: Reynolds et al., 2022. Diabetologia. 15 min post-meal: -1.5 mmol/L glucose spike. [Evidence: B]
- **Berberine**: Liang et al., 2019. J Clin Endocrinol Metab. Meta-analysis: -0.47% HbA1c. [Evidence: B]
- **Time-restricted eating**: de Cabo & Mattson, 2019. N Engl J Med. Improved insulin sensitivity. [Evidence: B]

### Insulin Resistance

- **HIIT**: Jelleyman et al., 2015. Diabetologia. Superior to moderate exercise for insulin sensitivity. [Evidence: A]
- **Inositol**: Unfer et al., 2017. Eur Rev Med Pharmacol Sci. 4g myo-inositol: improved HOMA-IR. [Evidence: B]

## Inflammation

### hs-CRP Reduction

- **Omega-3**: Calder, 2017. Eur J Pharmacol. EPA/DHA: 20-30% CRP reduction. [Evidence: A]
- **Exercise**: Fedewa et al., 2017. Med Sci Sports Exerc. Meta-analysis: -0.63 mg/L CRP. [Evidence: A]
- **Sleep**: Irwin et al., 2016. Sleep Med Rev. <6h sleep: 1.5x CRP elevation. [Evidence: B]

## Vitamins & Minerals

### Vitamin D

- **D3 supplementation**: Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline, 2024. 4000-5000 IU/day for deficiency. [Evidence: A]
- **K2 co-supplementation**: Knapen et al., 2013. Osteoporosis Int. MK-7 200mcg: improved calcium metabolism. [Evidence: B]

## Hormones

### Testosterone (Male)

- **Resistance training**: Vingren et al., 2010. Sports Med. Compound lifts: acute +15-20% T. [Evidence: A]
- **Sleep**: Leproult & Van Cauter, 2011. JAMA. 5h vs 8h sleep: -10-15% T. [Evidence: A]
- **Zinc + Magnesium**: Prasad et al., 1996. Nutrition. Zinc supplementation in mild deficiency: +12% T. [Evidence: B]

## Uric Acid

### Uric Acid Reduction

- **Purine/fructose reduction**: ACR 2020 Guidelines for Management of Gout. [Evidence: A]
- **Tart cherry**: Schlesinger et al., 2012. Arthritis Res Ther. 500mg extract: -1.5 mg/dL UA. [Evidence: B]
- **Hydration**: Neogi et al., 2015. Ann Rheum Dis. 2.5L+/day: 40% lower gout risk. [Evidence: B]

## Biological Age Algorithms

### PhenoAge

- Levine, 2018. "An epigenetic biomarker of aging for lifespan and healthspan." Aging, 10(4), 573-591.
- Uses 9 clinical chemistry biomarkers + chronological age
- Validated against NHANES III mortality data (n=11,432)
- Gompertz proportional hazard model

### KDM (Klemera-Doubal Method)

- Klemera & Doubal, 2006. "A new approach to the concept and computation of biological age." Mech Ageing Dev, 127(3), 240-248.
- Levine, 2013. "Modeling the rate of senescence." PLoS One, 8(6), e65874.
- NHANES III biomarker regression parameters
- Requires minimum 3 biomarkers for meaningful estimate
